About the simulation

This is a teaching tool, not a forecast. Numbers are calibrated to feel like Reisman's narrative. Four mechanisms from the book drive the game:

  1. Cochrane's loop — adoption raises yields, diffusion raises supply, supply outpaces demand, prices fall, laggards must adopt, scale, or exit.
  2. Anticipatory adoption — trees take ~4 years to bear and ~20 to remove. The forecast itself drives planting before any actual crash.
  3. Marketing as collective technology — a USDA-style levy funds advertising that lifts demand. Lifted demand attracts new entrants. The strategy backfires.
  4. Input lock-in — intensive irrigation triggers salt accumulation. Pesticides erode pollinator health, raising honeybee rentals. The "needs" you accept compound.

    There is no win condition. That is the point. The treadmill structures the field of play. You can survive it, get rich and crash, or stay small at the margin. None of these are failures of will — they are the shape of agrarian capitalism Reisman is mapping. (p. 14–16)
